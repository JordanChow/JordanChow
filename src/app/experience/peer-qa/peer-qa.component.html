I started my co-op career at the University Waterloo with Quality Assurance Specialist at PEER Group. At PEER Group, they specialize in automation software for semiconductor manufacturers, so it was a very relevant and interesting job in an extremely important industry. During my 4 months as a QA Specialist, I gained exposure to various testing technologies, Azure DevOps, and got to work on client projects.


Introduction

Firstly, for my orientation I became familiar with using Azure DevOps and the equipment automation and control framework product (PTO) we had at PEER Group. To finish off my orientation, I was tasked with vigorously testing a WPF application, including creating a test plan, executing test cases, and bug verification. I worked closely with the developer that created this application to resolve bugs and achieve a functioning product.


My Contributions to PEER Group

PG Apps

PG Apps is PEER Group's internal website that is used company wide. I began by setting up the QA environment on a VM to begin testing. Here, I applied my skills to write a test plan and test features across the website. I performed lots of adhoc testing and bug verification, while creating new builds. This experience got me used to the general QA process and I was able to work alongside developers to resolve issues.

Automated Testing Research

In between transferring projects, I took the initiative to investigate viable technologies PEER Group could use to help automate their UI testing of WPF applications. I created a document to record my findings and presented a detailed proposal to find the most suitable option for PEER Group down the line. These findings were presented to senior software developer managers, who were impressed by the level of research completed.

PG Apps - Engineering Utility

My next task was to test a new utility for PG Apps - a tool to automate the process of importing requirements and task work items from a Word document to Azure DevOps and export test cases to an Excel file. This was an extremely valuable asset for the engineering team, so I ensured the utility was functioning as required as the sole QA, testing 100+ documents and numerous edge cases using Microsoft Test Manager (MTM). I provided complete documentation and support for the usage of the utility and it was then launched company wide and has aided in project ramp up speed greatly.

Client Projects

The name of the projects are not disclosed due to privacy for PEER Group. I worked on two client projects, revolving around the PEER Tool Orchestrator (PTO) product. This product was a framework to help integrate tooling into different environments, accompanied by a GUI for easy customization. Here I spent the bulk of my co-op, testing the tool and its various components. This involved writing automated test scripts with the PEER Factory Acceptance Tester (PFAT), creating the test plan for a future iteration and presenting it to the project leads, and leveraging Equipment Information Bridge (EIB) server and SECSIM Pro+ to test equipment to host connectivity.

These client projects gave me a great opportunity to work in a team setting alongside with other QA, developers, and PMs. It was fascinating learning about these new tools that were extremely intricate and becoming comfortable with the development lifecycle and process. Finally to conclude, I was given an outstanding work term rating which signifies I made a unique contribution and had an exceptional performance with the company.
